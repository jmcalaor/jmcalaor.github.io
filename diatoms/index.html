Fine-Scale Morphology of Diatoms

Louis Stokes Alliances for Minority Participation (LSAMP) Program - Summer 2015

My first introduction to formal research was with Dr. Christopher Lobban at the University of Guam. We worked with a group of diatoms that were historically messy in taxonomy, Nitzschia. Within this group are conopeate species. We used a scanning-electron microscope to detect ultrastructural variation in this subset. From morphological differences, we named and described 14 new species, primarily from Guam and one from Costa Rica.

This was the first time I saw a project through and is my first publication. The results from this summer were also my first presentation at a national conference, the 2015 AISES Conference, where I won 5th place in student presentations. This experience got me excited about scientific research and sent me on my journey to the scientist I am today.
